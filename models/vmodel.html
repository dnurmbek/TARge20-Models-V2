V-model: Advantages and Disadvantages

Advantages Disadvantages
☆ Simple and easy to use. ★ Very rigid and least flexible.
☆ Testing activities like planning, test designing happens well before coding. This saves a lot of time. Hence higher chance of success over the waterfall model. ★ Software is developed during the implementation phase, so no early prototypes of the software are produced.
☆ Works well for small projects where requirements are easily understood. ★ If any changes happen in midway, then the test documents along with requirement documents has to be updated.
